TWITTER ANALYSIS: BBC MUSIC AWARDS

Introduction

The following analysis focuses on the Twitter traffic developed during the BBC Music Awards 2016.

The event happened on the 12th of December 2016 between 20.30 and 22.30 and was broadcasted live on the BBC.

To study the behaviour of the audience, I decided to scrape the hashtag #BBCMusicAwards from 18.00 to 23.30 and it resulted in almost 25.000 tweets.

The idea was to inspect the attitude of the public, to see if there is a connection between the amount of tweets addressed to a certain artist and his victory or his appearance on stage.

The intent was also to demonstrate who and what are the users tweeting about, who is the favourite celebrity of the event and which episodes during the night raised more response.

Furthermore, I focused on the BBC British Artist nominees ‚Äì Adele, Coldplay, David Bowie, Calvin Harris and Jess Glynne - and the performers of the night ‚Äì The 1975, Craig David, Emeli Sand√©, John Legend, Izzy Bizu, Robbie Williams and Zara Larsson ‚Äì linked to the #BBCMusicAwards hashtag.

I counted how many times each of them have been named or addressed in tweets related to the event and consequently discovered which artist is the Twitter favourite.

Adam Lambert case study

Adam Lambert is a 34 years old singer who became famous thanks to American Idol.

He released three very successful albums and in 2011 he started to collaborate with the historical rock band Queens as lead vocalist.
He was also one of the judges at American Idol and X-Factor Australia in 2015 and 2016.

During the event, he hit the stage only once, to present the BBC British Artist of the Year prize, but his presence on Twitter remains steady since before the start, when during a press interview he disclosed that he is working on new material.

Even if his presence was short, without him the result of this scrape would have been completely different.

His fans are not only part of the top ten tweeters and retweeters, but he also got tweeted more than any other artist who performed during the night and more than any other nominee or winner.

Performers and nominees' tweet volume reached higher peaks over the show, which corresponded to their awards or to their performances, while Adam Lambert Twitter presence was regular all over the event, with a small peak when he announces Coldplay victory.
